The Storyteller

Vinay Varanasi is not just a storyteller; he is a bridge between the ancient and the avant-garde. Trained as an architect with a Masters from Bauhaus-Universität Weimar, Vinay brings a structural elegance to the fluid world of Indian mythology.

Founder of Shuka

As the founder of Shuka, Vinay works to revive immortal stories through curated events, productions, and workshops. He believes that stories are gateways to new realms of understanding, not just entertainment.

A Multifaceted Artist

  • Lyricist: He pens pallavis in Sanskrit, Telugu, and Kannada, performed by maestros like Ranjani-Gayatri.
  • Visual Artist: His paintings explore Puranic symbolism, often serving as the backdrop for his narratives.
  • Design Researcher: Co-founder of Unbind, he merges design thinking with traditional wisdom.
"I want to create a space where we can question the epics, not to debunk them, but to understand their relevance to our modern emotions—our dejection, our surrender, and our love."

Kumaram: The Son of Shiva & Shakti

A visually stunning exploration of Lord Kartikeya (Murugan). This production delves into the concept of "Shanmukha"—the six-faced god—and the union of power and grace. Vinay deconstructs the symbolism of the Vel (spear) and the peacock, blending narrative with high-energy musical accompaniment.

#Kartikeya #Power #DivineUnion

Annapoorna: The Goddess of All

In this soulful rendition, Vinay takes us to the ghats of Kashi (Varanasi). He narrates the story of Goddess Annapoorna, not just as the provider of food, but as the source of all sustenance—physical, emotional, and spiritual. The story highlights the moment Shiva himself becomes a beggar before the Mother.

#Kashi #MotherGoddess #Sustenance
